Our Purpose

1. To provide a meeting facility to approved twelve-step fellowships that help people recover from alcoholism, drug addiction, compulsion, or other behavioral problems.

2. To provide a welcoming environment for people seeking recovery through the twelve-step fellowships

Offering Twelve-Step Services Since 1990

The Greenbelt Step Club, Inc., located in the heart of Historic Greenbelt, Maryland, which the U.S. Department of Interior designated as a National Historic Landmark in 1997, has continuously offered twelve-step meetings and helped thousands in their quest for a path of recovery since 1990. From its beginning, the Club has been available to any approved twelve-step group interested in offering meetings to people recovering from alcoholism, drug addiction, compulsion, or other behavioral problems.

Presently, the Club offers some forty twelve-step meetings per week, representing Alcoholics Anonymous (AA), Adult Children Anonymous (ACA), Chemically Dependent Anonymous (CDA), Dual Recovery Anonymous (DRA), and Narcotics Anonymous (NA). Click Twelve-Step Fellowships in the website's menu in the left column to learn about these fellowships. To see which meetings are held daily at the Club, click Find a Meeting in the menu.

The twelve-step fellowships represented at the Club operate autonomously and independently of Club influence except the Club is “landlord” of the meeting premises and provider of the services. The Club does not speak for nor represent the twelve-step groups, nor does it participate in AA General Service Office activities. A Fellowship Steering Committee composed of representatives of the Club’s twelve-step groups provides oversight responsibility to ensure the various groups’ autonomy, to assist individual meeting groups as necessary, and to deal with the Club’s Board of Directors on matters pertaining to the groups.

Official membership in the Club is not required to attend the meetings or to participate in the Club’s social activities. However, membership is encouraged as it entitles a person to vote in elections and to have a say-so in policy matters affecting the Club.
